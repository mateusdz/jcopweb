Introduction

The (Hellenic) National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA, hereafter the Authority) is the national competent authority for cybersecurity in Greece, according to the NIS Directive and national Law 4577/2018. The NCSA, which operates under the Ministry of Digital Governance, functions as a General Directorate covering the areas of cybersecurity strategic planning, prevention and protection, business continuity and coordination. The NCSA is recruited with public sector officials, including cybersecurity experts, IT engineers, legal experts and public management officials. NCSA has established strong collaborations with the Governmental CERT, the National CSIRT, as well as other competent authorities. Furthermore, the Authority collaborates with EU agencies, other authorities and organisations, such as the NIS Cooperation Group, ENISA and the EU CERT Network, and participates in the relevant discussions and works. Finally, the NCSA also is engaged in international collaborations and Europeanfunded projects, like JCOP.

    The Authority's high-level mission is described as follows:
  • Supervision of national cybersecurity policy.
  • Supporting national efforts in incident handling & threat response.
  • Coordination of national cybersecurity stakeholders.
  • Cooperation with national and international actors.
  • Promotion of a cyber-secure culture in Greece.

Figure 1: National Cybersecurity Strategic Objectives

JCOP is a European project driven by EU's Cybersecurity's strategy and guidelines, which aspires to facilitate shared situational awareness, preparedness and coordinated incident response with the cybersecurity structures and mechanisms established under the Directive on security of network and information systems (NIS Directive).

Figure 2: Pillars of JCOP project

A summary of JCOP regarding drivers, scope, and objectives is presented below:

    Drivers:
  • EU's Cybersecurity Strategy1 (adopted Dec. 2020) focuses on collective resilience against cyber threats
  • “Cyber Blueprint”2 - European Commission Recommendation on Coordinated Response to Large Scale Cybersecurity Incidents and Crises
  • EU CyCLONe3 - Cyber Crisis Liaison Organisation Network
    Scope:
  • Design, develop and deliver a Joint Cybersecurity Operations Platform (JCOP) tailored to the needs of EU Member State authorities entrusted with cybersecurity, providing a holistic cybersecurity operations solution.
    Aims:
  • Create a model Southeast Europe Coordinated Response Cluster, encompassing authorities of Greece and Cyprus, with validation from the Norwegian competent authority.
  • Demonstrate in practice the capacity- and coordination- building impact of JCOP at the national (between national JCOP instances) and the EU level (between JCOP instances and EU cybersecurity bodies, such as the CSIRTs network, CERT-EU, and ENISA).
  • Establish JCOP as a prototypical implementation of the “Cyber Blueprint” and a reference that can be applied to additional Member States.

Given the vision and the goals of the National Cyber Security Strategy of Greece for the period 2020-2025 (Figure 1), it becomes apparent that a European project like JCOP is not only facilitating part of NCSA's mission as a means of collaboration with other European countries, academia and research centres, but it also supports various national strategic objectives. Thus, before even getting into further analysis, it is self-evident that the potential added value of participating to European projects is directly linked with the mission and objectives of the Authority.

Moving forward to a more specific benefits estimation, we will focus on the three pillars of JCOP's toolbox and the new equipment, which was part of JCOP implementation, and demonstrate how they relate to the Authority's mission and the national objectives.

Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)

Cybersecurity Threat Intelligence (CTI) is an upcoming cybersecurity area of interest as an answer to a complex and evolving threat landscape and is inseparable from a National Authority's cybersecurity strategic planning and security awareness policies. JCOP project includes technical sessions regarding CTI sharing models (STIX & MISP), tracking of tactical, operational and strategic intelligence sources, both open source and proprietary, and finally implementation and training on a widespread CTI platform.

Alerting & Incident Response (AIR)

NIS cooperation groups, CyCLONe, ENISA and other international and European collaboration groups, rely heavily on information sharing and common approaches at incident response procedures. Incident management optimization, through international/ European collaborations is a critical national strategic objective. JCOP project in order to develop a proper Alert & Incident Response (AIR) toolbox, is analysing all the Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) regarding National authorities AIR ecosystem and it is an opportunity to review and optimize relevant forms and communication lines.

Cyber Security Operations training

Cybersecurity Operations training is not just an internal issue but is also driven by the EU's Cybersecurity strategy and the Council's recommendations for achieving collective resilience against cyber threats (namely, the Coordinated Response to Large Scale Cybersecurity Incidents and Crises). To achieve the EU's guidelines, capacity building and security awareness raising are elevated from local National Issues to global international/ European collaboration fields. JCOP as a Joint Cooperation Platform, defines specific training scenarios and provides a useful collaborative training tool according to EU's directions.

State-of-the-art equipment

Part of the JCOP project is the addition of new technical infrastructure in National Authorities' premises to enhance their SOC capabilities. After consultation among the involved parties a new video wall control system with advanced management capabilities is added to the Authority's Infrastructures together with proper hosting equipment for the JCOP portal.

The video wall control system is placed between the JCOP hosting and administration systems (Server & Workstations) and the video wall TV screens to provide the ability to manage incoming visual information from JCOP instances/ dashboards and distribute them to any preferred layout to the video wall.

    With the suggested addition, JCOP infrastructure will be able to:
  • fully integrate with the existing monitoring infrastructure of the Authority,
  • provide to the Authority access to state-of-the-art equipment for usage and further evaluation,
  • ensure the addition of JCOP instances to current monitoring and evaluating activities, from day-one of the deployment.

Summarizing the added value of the JCOP project at the National Cyber Security Authority , it is clear that European projects can be a key element for National Authorities to achieve their mission and goals and they can actually improve the effectiveness of their daily cybersecurity operations.
